Incoming Postdoctoral Researcher, NYU
Former Ph.D. Candidate, Chemical & Biological Engineering, University of Colorado Boulder

I work on microhydrodynamics, colloids, and electrokinetics — building theoretical and computational models to understand transport and dynamics of microparticles in complex fluids and confined geometries.

Research themes

  • Microhydrodynamics & Colloids: Particle motion, fluid–structure interactions, inertial & non-inertial regimes.
  • Electrokinetics & Phoretic Transport: Diffusiophoresis, electrophoresis, self-phoretic propulsion of colloids under applied or self-generated fields.
  • Multiscale Transport & Modeling: Bridging microscale physics to macroscopic transport — with applications in soft matter, fluid mixing, and charged-particle transport.

Research overview

My work sits at the interface of fluid mechanics, electrokinetics, and colloidal science. I employ analytical theory, asymptotic / slender-body methods, and computational simulations to derive mobility laws and predict trajectories and transport behavior of particles in complex fluids.

Recently I have also explored multiscale aspects — extending microscale insights toward mesoscale / continuum-level behavior relevant to transport, separation, and electrokinetic applications.
