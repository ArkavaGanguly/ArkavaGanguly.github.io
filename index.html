Arkava Ganguly

Incoming Postdoctoral Researcher, NYU
Ph.D., Chemical & Biological Engineering, University of Colorado Boulder

I work on microhydrodynamics, colloids, and electrokinetics—building theoretical and computational models to understand transport and dynamics of microparticles in complex fluids and confined geometries.

Research themes

  • Microhydrodynamics & Colloids: Particle motion, fluid–structure interactions.
  • Electrokinetics & Phoretic transport: Diffusiophoresis, electrophoresis, self-phoresis.
  • Multiscale transport & modeling: Connecting microscale physics to mesoscale transport.

Research overview

My work sits at the interface of fluid mechanics, electrokinetics, and colloidal science. I employ analytical theory, asymptotic and slender-body methods, and computational simulations to derive mobility laws and predict particle trajectories and transport in complex fluids.
